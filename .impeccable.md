## Design Context

### Users
All ages — from young adults managing their first apartment to older family members doing weekly grocery runs. Households ranging from singles to couples to families. Users are budget-aware and want to feel in control of their spending. Context: primarily mobile, light environments (supermarket aisles, kitchen counters).

### Brand Personality
**One sentence**: "Your shopping list, budget, and recipes — all in one calm place."
**Three words**: Grounded · Organized · Nourishing

### Emotional Goal
Calm and organized. The app should feel like a reliable, uncluttered tool — not flashy, not stressful. Users should feel confident and in control when they open it.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light mode primary
- **Visual tone**: Clean, warm-neutral, approachable. Not clinical or cold. Not playful/toy-like either. Think: a well-organized kitchen counter — functional, pleasant, trusted.
- **Color palette**: Forest green (#2B7A5C) as primary CTA anchor. Warm neutral backgrounds (not stark white). Per-category accent palette (food/dairy/meat/veggies/fruit/drinks/household/personal) with light and dark mode variants. Muted greens and ambers for budget/food feedback.
- **Typography**: Legible at all ages. Clear hierarchy. No decorative display fonts — readability over personality.
- **Anti-references**: Nothing that looks like a fintech dashboard (too cold/clinical). Nothing that looks like a food delivery app (too promotional/loud). No neon, no glassmorphism.

### Design Principles
1. **Calm by default** — reduce visual noise; let whitespace breathe; avoid competing focal points on the same screen
2. **Legible for all ages** — minimum 16px body text, generous touch targets (48px+), high contrast text
3. **Budget as friend, not judge** — progress indicators should feel supportive, not alarming; use warm ambers before aggressive reds
4. **Unified flow** — shopping → budget → cooking should feel like one connected journey, not three separate apps stitched together
5. **Trusted utility** — every screen should look like it was designed for repeat daily use, not a one-time wow moment
